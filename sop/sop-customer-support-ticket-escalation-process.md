# SOP - Customer Support Ticket Escalation Process

## 1. Purpose

This SOP defines the process for handling and escalating customer support tickets in TaskFlow to ensure timely resolution and consistent service quality.

## 2. Scope

This procedure applies to all customer support tickets submitted through the TaskFlow support system.

## 3. Roles and Responsibilities

**Level 1 Support (L1):**

- Reviews incoming tickets
- Provides initial response
- Attempts basic issue resolution

**Level 2 Support (L2):**

- Handles complex or unresolved issues
- Investigates technical problems

**Engineering Team:**

- Resolves product-related bugs
- Implements fixes when required

## 4. Tools Required

- TaskFlow Support Dashboard
- Email system
- Internal communication tools (e.g., Slack or Teams)

## 5. Procedure

### Step 1: Receive Ticket

1. A support ticket is submitted through the support form.
2. The system generates a unique ticket ID.

### Step 2: Initial Review (L1 Support)

1. Open the ticket in the support dashboard.
2. Review the issue description and attachments.
3. Categorize the issue (e.g., login issue, task error, billing issue).

### Step 3: First-Level Resolution

1. Check the knowledge base for a solution.
2. Respond to the customer with troubleshooting steps.
3. Update the ticket status to “In Progress.”

### Step 4: Escalation to Level 2

Escalate the ticket if:

- The issue is not resolved within 24 hours
- The issue is technical or complex

To escalate:

1. Assign the ticket to Level 2 support.
2. Add notes explaining the issue and actions taken.
3. Update the ticket status to “Escalated.”

### Step 5: Level 2 Investigation

1. Analyze the issue in detail.
2. Reproduce the problem if possible.
3. Contact the engineering team if required.

### Step 6: Resolution

1. Provide a solution or workaround.
2. Respond to the customer with clear instructions.
3. Update the ticket status to “Resolved.”

### Step 7: Closure

1. Confirm that the issue is resolved.
2. Close the ticket.

## 6. Exceptions or Escalation

- If the issue is critical (system outage), escalate immediately to engineering.
- If customer response is delayed, send a follow-up within 48 hours.
